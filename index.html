CH.01

Location & Vibe

Bristol is a port city that builds without permission.

Birthplace of sound systems and pirate radio, home to a street-art scene the rest of the world admires, and a place where culture leaks out of basements and spreads globally. Independent to its core, with more indie venues, record shops and pubs per square mile than just about anywhere in the UK; and small enough to cross on foot, big enough to get lost in.

Expect a wonderful harbourside, painted houses stacked up hillsides, a serious music underground, and a food scene punching way above its weight.

Do you need a visa?

Good news for many of you: probably not.

Visitors from the US, EU, Canada, Australia, Japan and many other countries can enter the UK as a standard visitor without a traditional visa, but most visa-free travellers now need an ETA (Electronic Travel Authorisation) before flying.

  • ETA

    Apply online via the official UK ETA app or GOV.UK before you travel. It’s quick and low-cost, and lasts multiple trips. Apply at least a few days ahead to be safe.

  • Full visitor visa

    If you need one (depending on your nationality), apply early through GOV.UK, processing can take a few weeks.

⚠️ Rules change and are nationality-specific. Always confirm your exact requirement on the official GOV.UK site before booking flights.

Need an invitation letter?

If your visa or ETA application requires supporting documents, we’ve got you covered. Forma can provide an official invitation letter confirming your participation, along with a draft program outlining the event dates, venue and activities to include with your application.

You’ll find the template and program draft ready to share with the authorities. Reach out early so you have everything in hand well before you apply.

Transportation

Getting from the airport

Bristol Airport (BRS) sits about 8 miles south of the city. There are no trains from the airport, so it’s bus or car:

Airport Flyer (A1 bus)

Cheapest
  • Around £8–£10 for a single
  • Runs 24/7, up to every 8 minutes
  • Connects to the City Centre, Bus Station and Temple Meads (main rail station)
  • Pay contactless on board or via the First Bus app

Uber / Bolt

Recommended
  • Download the app before you arrive
  • Roughly £25–£35 to the city centre
  • About 20–30 minutes
  • Easier with luggage and after late flights
✈️ Flying into another UK airport? Heathrow and Gatwick connect to Bristol by National Express / FlixBus coach (arrives at Bristol Bus & Coach Station) or by train via London. London → Bristol Temple Meads is roughly 1h40 by direct train.

If you land at Heathrow:

  • Option 1: You take the Elizabeth line to Paddington Station and book a train ( Great Western Railway line ) on trainline to Bristol ( Bristol Temple Meads )
  • Second Option: Book a bus to Bristol ( Flixbus or National Express).

If you land at Gatwick

  • Take the GWR to Reading, change trains and take the next station to Bristol Temple Meads

CH.05

Travel essentials

Just a few things to make your stay smooth:

🔌

Power adapter

UK Type G plug (three rectangular pins), 230V. Bring a universal adapter, UK sockets are unlike almost everywhere else.

📱

SIM / eSIM

Easiest is an eSIM (Airalo, Saily, etc.) before you land. Otherwise grab a pay-as-you-go SIM (EE, Three, Vodafone) at the airport or in town. WiFi is everywhere but data is clutch for maps and rides.

£

Currency

British Pound (£ / GBP). Cards and Apple/Google Pay are accepted basically everywhere, you can get by almost cashless. Carry a little cash for markets and small venues.

🌤️

Weather

Late June / early July is Bristol summer, mild and bright, but changeable. Highs around 19–22°C (66–72°F), evenings dropping to ~10–12°C (50–54°F). There’s always a chance of a shower.

👕 Pack layers + a light rain jacket. Sunny morning, drizzle by lunch, golden evening, that’s a normal Bristol day. Comfortable shoes are a must; you’ll be walking.

FAQ

How much does a ticket cost?

Each ticket costs 1 SOL, and it’s free for British nationals who live in the UK.

What does the ticket include?

Your ticket covers access to all Forma Bristol programming: workshops, talks, performances and curated cultural experiences. It also includes select meals, community gatherings, and materials for select workshops. Travel, accommodation and most meals outside of programming are not included.

How do I get to Bristol?

Fly into Bristol Airport (BRS) and take the Airport Flyer bus (~£8–£10) or an Uber/Bolt (~£25–£35) to the centre. Flying into Heathrow or Gatwick instead? Connect by direct train (London → Bristol Temple Meads, ~1h40) or National Express / FlixBus coach. See the Transportation section above for details.

Can I get help finding accommodation?

Yes. We’ve secured attendee rates at three central hotels via Visit Bristol, plus a discount at PREMIER SUITES serviced apartments (code FORMA10). See the Housing section above. For group bookings or issues, email bookings@meetbristolbathres.co.uk.

Do I need a visa?

Many travellers won’t need a full visa but will need an ETA before flying, check GOV.UK for your nationality. If you need an invitation letter for your application, we provide one along with a draft program. See the Visa section above.
